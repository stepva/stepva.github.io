I finally got to visit a specialty cafÃ© in Vienna, Austria - for my first local roast I decided to visit Jonas Reindl, one of the best-rated and most famous coffee roasteries in Vienna. What surprised me there was a relatively small range of coffees to choose from - only two filter roasts if I recall correctly - so I decided to try this Dimtu Village. Both we and the place were a bit in a rush, so sadly I didn't really get to discuss the coffee with the baristas.

Farmed and produced by Aklilu Kassa, a member of a family with a long coffee tradition, together with his son Biniyam Aklilu, this roast takes its name from the Dimtu Coffee Washing Station located in the highlands of Western Guji, in the large Oromio region, standing relatively high at 2150 MASL. Interestingly and sadly, I can't really work out if it is the father, with his Nardos Coffee exporting company, or the son with his new Gujoo Trading company, who is the main person behind this coffee. Jonas Reindl seems to be mixing the two with Aklilu Kassa as the farmer on the package, but focusing on Biniyam Aklilu on their website... Nevertheless, the family story is quite interesting, and locating the origin has been a challenge.

What can be said with fair certainty, however, is the washed processing used for these beans. They themselves come from the classic mixed heirloom variety, a common name for wild varieties in Ethiopia. Because of the altitude, these beans were smaller and the drying process takes a lot longer than usual, 22 to 25 days, due to the cold weather. The beans were also visibly smaller in the final roasted package.

My first impression was the strong fruity smell. The official taste profile says tangerines, vanilla, and dried stone fruits, together with the smell very much typical for the heirloom. Around the same time that I was starting with this coffee, James Hoffman published his new V60 1-cup technique, just in time for me, so I immediately tried it - with great success. It was my main method of preparation for this coffee and I really liked it, it was very consistent. When colder, I tasted the citruses (perhaps tangerines?). In the end, I ended up with a slightly finer grind (22 on my 1zpresso Q2) and I have been constantly amazed by how consistent the taste was. Nothing extra special, just a really good coffee with a citrusy finish.

An interesting observation which I have not yet confirmed officially, a lot of coffee powder stays on the inside of the grinder after grinding, as if the coffee was a bit wet - I assume this comes from the fact that it was processed using the washed method.
