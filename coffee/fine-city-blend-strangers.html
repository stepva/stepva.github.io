Jordan brought this from his hometown, Norwich, where Strangers Coffee Company roasts and brews and improves the British coffee scene. I haven't visited yet, but it's definitely on my list now - they also have nice merch and easy brew guides on their website. This particular coffee is their award-winning Fine City Blend of American and African beans.

It includes Colombian and Ethiopian coffee of Caturra and Heirloom varieties from around 2000 MASL, mixing washed and natural processing. The official flavour profile states red berries, chocolate, and orange citrus, and especially the red berries with chocolate were very nice and strong there.

We almost exclusively used this Fine City Blend to prepare our "fakespresso" in AeroPress, using our favorite recipe from the Aeromatic app by Tim Wendelboe, producing a stronger cup of coffee (apparently similar to a moka pot). It's an easy recipe, with my Q2 grinder set at 1.0.2 for 20g of coffee, topped with 125 of boiling water, stirred 3 times and immediately pressed. It was really surprisingly good and I enjoyed it very much.
