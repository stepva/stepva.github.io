During the second half of the summer I spent almost all weekends in Vrdy, usually mowing the lawn and cycling. On this occasion I decided to ride to Pardubice (via some nice climbs around Lichnice) and visit my favorite local coffee place - PutovnÃ­ PraÅ¾Ã­rna. There I ordered my summer classic - espresso tonic, a delicious cake, and also decided to buy beans for home. Knowing that I want something not-so-wild so that my mom could enjoy it as well, the barista recommended this Ethiopia.

A classic Ethiopian heirloom JARC variety, developed by the Jimma Agricultura Research Center, it comes from a Guji region and quite a high altitude, at 2200-2500 MASL. The coffee trees are growing in a semi-wild environment in a protected forest, resulting in more unique varietals. This coffee was fully washed and has a taste profile of elderberry, cocoa and peach - not too fruity and sour, but still interesting. And luckily, my coffee-conservative mom really liked it, so we V60-ed it quite quickly together during September.
