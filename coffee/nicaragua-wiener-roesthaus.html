Another one from a nearby classic, Winer RÃ¶sthaus. Again, I didn't have that much time to play with it, but I did note down very visually dark beans and a very fruity smell - which was a surprise since otherwise, the coffee was more on the chocolate-bitter side.

It comes from the Miraflor Esteli reserve from just 950-1350 MASL, and is a washed mix of Catuai and Caturra varietas. The official flavour profile includes almond aroma and citruses, with chocolate notes, slightly floral with full and strong finish.

I used mainly the classic V60 with a classic Hoffmann 1cup technique, and for the first time I also started measuring the temperature of the water - or rather the time after boiling. It took some tries to get to a finer grind (1.9.0 on 1zpresso Q2) and optimal 2-minute water boil time, but it became a very consistent and good 300ml cup from 18g with 2:30 minutes brew time. I would call it a success and pleasure!

I also tried it once with AeroPress, using the Blue Bottle recipe from the Aeromatic app, which resulted in a lighter, more "milky" cup I would say, definitely not bitter either.
