Cuppings are amazing! And during my first three weeks back in Prague, I went to three different ones. Each hosted by EMA Espresso bar, each presenting different coffees and roasters. This time it was Friedhats, Amsterdam-based micro roastery focusing on special and interesting coffee and presenting it in their famous pill-dose-resembling packages. After tasting and slurping 8 different filters, I bought one for my colleague and one for myself - nice and fruity Colombia.

In a native Colombian dialect the name of the variety, tabi, means "good", and it really is good - it's a variety known for exceptional and great coffee. A hybrid between Typica, Bourbon, and Timor, I could not find it in the Varieties Catalog from World Coffee Research, as the search resulted in a Pacamara variety, also a Bourbon-Typica group variety, probably having a lot in common with Tabi.

Nevertheless, this coffee comes from the farm of Alvaro Mora, in Palestina, Huila region, at 1680 MASL, which he apparently inherited from his mother. It has silky "notes", raspberry, dark chocolate and vanilla. I clearly seem to have a thing for raspberry, since the second one I liked and picked for my colleague Petr also had this fruit in its profile. I remember the cups I brewed at home having very strong and nice smell, and the taste was also great, nice and fruity, not too sour... I was happy with this one.
